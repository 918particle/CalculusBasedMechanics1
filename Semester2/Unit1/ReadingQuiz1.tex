\section{Memory Bank}

\begin{itemize}
\item $\Delta U = q\Delta V$ ... Relationship between change in potential energy, charge, and voltage.
\item $V(x) = E x + V_0$ ... Linear relationship between voltage, electric field, and distance between two charge plates to form a capacitor.
\item $Q = C\Delta V$ ... Relationship between stored charge, capacitance ($C$), and voltage.  Capacitance has the unit of 1 Farad, or 1 C V$^{-1}$.
\item $I = \Delta Q / \Delta t$ ... The relationship between current ($I$), charge, and time.  The unit of current is 1 Amp (A), or 1 C s$^{-1}$.  
\item $\Delta U = \frac{1}{2}C \Delta V^2$ ... The energy stored in a capacitor.
\item $C = \epsilon_0 A/d$ ... The capacitance of a parallel-plate capacitor with area $A$ and plate separation $d$.
\end{itemize}

\section{Capacitors and Current}

\begin{enumerate}
\item (a) Suppose we allow 5 mA of current to flow onto a capacitor for 5 seconds.  How much charge is stored in the capacitor? (b) After 5 seconds, the capacitor voltage is measured to be 5.0 V.  What is the capacitance? \\ \vspace{3cm}
\item What capacitance is needed to store 3.00 $\mu$C of charge at a voltage of 120 V? \\ \vspace{1cm}
\item (a) Find the capacitance of a parallel plate capacitor having plates of area 4.00 cm$^2$ that are separated by 0.100 mm. (b) Filling the space between the plates with Teflon increases the value of $\epsilon_0$ by a factor of 2.1.  If the capacitor in part (a) was filled with Teflon, what would the new capacitance be? \\ \vspace{2cm}
\item Consider Fig. \ref{fig:1}.  Suppose a battery is connected to the top and bottom of the circuit.  The first two capacitors are \textit{in parallel}, because they would split the current, but share the voltage. Their total capacitance is
\begin{equation}
C_{\rm pair} = C_1 + C_2
\end{equation}
(a) What is the total capcitance of the top two capacitors? (b) The top pair of capacitors are \textit{in series} with the lower one, because they share the current but split the voltage.  The total capacitance is
\begin{equation}
C_{\rm tot}^{-1} = C_{\rm pair}^{-1} + C_{\rm 3}^{-1}
\end{equation}
(b) What is $C_{\rm tot}$? \\ \vspace{2cm}
\end{enumerate}
